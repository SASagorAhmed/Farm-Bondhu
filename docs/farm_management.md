# FarmBondhu Farm Management Guide

FarmBondhu Farm Management is the operational workspace for farmers, farm owners, livestock managers, poultry managers, dairy operators, and farm staff. It helps organize farms, animals, sheds, feed, health, production, finances, mortality, and sales records in one structured system.

Farm management is one major service inside the FarmBondhu green SaaS ecosystem. A user can manage a farm here, then use GreenBondhu Marketplace, VetBondhu, Community, AI assistance, or admin support when those services are needed.

---

## Purpose

Farm Management exists to help users run day-to-day farm operations with better records and clearer decisions.

It supports:

- Farm and location organization
- Animal and batch tracking
- Shed capacity and status tracking
- Feed planning and feed records
- Health and treatment history
- Production records for milk, eggs, meat, and other outputs
- Financial records for expenses, income, and profit review
- Mortality records for animal-loss tracking
- Sales history for livestock, products, or farm outputs
- Links to VetBondhu, Community, marketplace, and AI help when needed

The goal is to reduce scattered paper records, missed health history, unclear costs, and delayed farm decisions.

---

## Why Farm Management Matters

Good farm records help owners answer important questions:

- How many animals are active?
- Which shed has available capacity?
- Which animals are sick or need follow-up?
- What feed is being used and how much does it cost?
- How much milk, egg, meat, or other production is being recorded?
- Which sales happened this month?
- What expenses are reducing profit?
- Which disease or mortality patterns are repeating?

Without organized records, owners may miss early health signs, duplicate medicine, lose production history, or make decisions without reliable data.

## Before Starting Setup

Before using Farm Management, prepare the basic farm information.

### Farm details

- Farm name
- Farm location
- Farm type: poultry, dairy, mixed, or other local category
- Number of sheds
- Main animal types
- Owner or responsible person

### Animal details

- Animal type: cow, goat, sheep, chicken, duck, pigeon, etc.
- Individual animal names or IDs when available
- Batch IDs for poultry or grouped animals
- Breed
- Age or approximate age
- Sex where relevant
- Health status
- Farm and shed assignment

### Operational records

Prepare available records for:

- Feed plan and feed inventory
- Vaccination and deworming history
- Previous disease and treatment history
- Production targets
- Daily milk, egg, meat, or other output
- Expenses and income
- Sales records
- Mortality or loss history

Starting with approximate records is acceptable. The system becomes more valuable as users update it regularly.

## Main Farm Management Features

### Farms

The farm record is the top-level workspace. It stores the farm name, location, type, shed count, and overall farm identity.

Use farms to separate different locations or operations. For example, a user may have one poultry farm and one dairy farm.

### Animals

Animal records track individual animals or batches.

Animal records can include:

- Animal type
- Tracking mode: individual or batch
- Name or batch ID
- Batch size
- Breed
- Age
- Health status
- Farm assignment

Use individual tracking for valuable animals such as cows, breeding animals, or pets. Use batch tracking for poultry, groups of goats, or animals managed together.

### Sheds

Shed records help organize housing and capacity.

Shed records can include:

- Shed name
- Farm assignment
- Animal type
- Capacity
- Current count
- Status: active, maintenance, empty, or similar operational status

Shed tracking helps prevent overcrowding and makes it easier to understand where animals are located.

### Feed

Feed records help track what animals eat and how feed affects cost, health, and production.

Useful feed data includes:

- Feed type
- Quantity
- Cost
- Feeding date
- Animal group or shed
- Supplier or marketplace source
- Notes about feed changes

Feed changes should be recorded because sudden changes can affect digestion, production, and disease risk.

### Health

Health records help track sickness, treatment, vaccination, deworming, injuries, and recovery.

Useful health data includes:

- Symptoms
- Date started
- Animal or batch affected
- Medicine or treatment given
- Vet advice or prescription
- Follow-up date
- Recovery status

If a health issue is serious, use VetBondhu or contact a local vet immediately.

### Production

Production records help users understand farm output.

Examples:

- Milk production
- Egg production
- Meat production
- Animal growth
- Hatch or breeding results
- Other farm outputs

Production records help compare performance across days, weeks, months, animals, sheds, or feed plans.

### Finances

Financial records help track cost, income, and profit.

Common records:

- Feed expenses
- Medicine expenses
- Labor cost
- Equipment cost
- Maintenance cost
- Product sales
- Animal sales
- Marketplace purchases
- Other income or expense

Good financial tracking helps owners see whether the farm is profitable.

### Mortality

Mortality records track animal deaths or losses.

Record:

- Date
- Animal or batch
- Suspected cause
- Symptoms before death
- Vet advice if any
- Disposal or safety action taken
- Related feed, disease, weather, or management notes

Mortality records are important for disease pattern detection and future prevention.

### Sales

Sales records track farm outputs sold to buyers or markets.

Examples:

- Milk sales
- Egg sales
- Meat sales
- Animal sales
- Fertilizer/manure sales
- Other farm products

Sales history helps compare income against production and expenses.

## Farm Setup Guide

Recommended setup order:

1. Create the farm record.
2. Add sheds or housing areas.
3. Add animals or batches.
4. Add feed records or feed plan.
5. Add known health history.
6. Start daily production records.
7. Add expenses and sales.
8. Review dashboard and reports regularly.

Do not wait for perfect data. Start with the most important records and improve over time.

---

## Daily Farm Workflow

A practical daily workflow can be:

1. Check animal behavior, eating, drinking, movement, and signs of illness.
2. Review shed condition, cleanliness, crowding, temperature, and ventilation.
3. Record feed given and any feed changes.
4. Record production such as milk, eggs, growth, or other output.
5. Log health issues immediately.
6. Record expenses and sales before they are forgotten.
7. Review notifications or reminders.
8. Ask Community for general learning questions.
9. Use VetBondhu for veterinary guidance when symptoms need professional review.

Daily records do not need to be long. Consistent small updates are more useful than rare large updates.

## Animal And Batch Tracking

### Individual tracking

Use individual tracking when each animal needs its own history.

Good for:

- Dairy cows
- Breeding animals
- Sick animals needing follow-up
- High-value livestock
- Pets or special-care animals

### Batch tracking

Use batch tracking when animals are managed as a group.

Good for:

- Poultry flocks
- Duck groups
- Goat groups
- Sheep groups
- Same-age feeding or production groups

Batch tracking should still include size, age, health status, shed, feed plan, mortality, and production history.

## Health And Emergency Flow

When an animal looks sick:

1. Record the symptom and time noticed.
2. Check eating, drinking, stool, breathing, temperature, and movement.
3. Note recent feed changes, weather changes, injuries, transport, or contact with sick animals.
4. Add photos or videos if safe.
5. Use VetBondhu for veterinary consultation when professional guidance is needed.
6. Contact the nearest local vet or clinic immediately for severe emergencies.

Emergency signs include:

- Severe breathing difficulty
- Heavy bleeding
- Poisoning
- Bloat or swollen abdomen
- Difficult birth
- Collapse or inability to stand
- Seizures
- Deep wounds or fractures
- Severe dehydration
- Rapid worsening with high fever

FarmBondhu records can help the vet understand history, but emergency treatment should not be delayed.

## Business And Profit Flow

Farm Management should help owners connect operations with business results.

Track:

- Feed cost

- Labor cost

Good records help convert farm work into better business decisions.

## Privacy And Ownership Rules

Farm records are private operational data.

Privacy expectations:

- Farm records should belong to the correct owner account.
- Animal records should not appear in another user's dashboard.
- Health and financial records should stay private.
- Admin/support access should be limited to authorized operational needs.
- Notifications should not leak farm activity to the wrong account.
- Shared community posts should not expose private farm details unless the owner chooses to share them.

Privacy is especially important for finances, disease history, production, and customer/sales records.

## Good Record-Keeping Examples

### Health record example

```text
Animal: Cow A-12
Issue: Not eating and mild fever
Date noticed: 2026-06-02
Symptoms: Low appetite, slow movement, temperature high
Action: Contacted VetBondhu, shared video and previous deworming date
Follow-up: Recheck appetite and temperature tomorrow
```

### Feed record example

```text
Group: Poultry Batch P-07
Feed: Starter feed
Quantity: 25 kg
Date: 2026-06-02
Note: New supplier, monitor stool and growth for 3 days
```

### Production record example

```text
Farm: Dairy Unit 1
Output: Milk
Quantity: 42 liters
Date: 2026-06-02
Note: Lower than usual; check feed and cow health
```

### Sales record example

```text
Product: Eggs
Quantity sold: 300 pieces
Buyer: Local shop
Income: 3600 BDT
Date: 2026-06-02
```
