# VetBondhu Guide

VetBondhu is FarmBondhu's animal-care and veterinary consultation service. It helps farmers, livestock owners, pet owners, and vets connect for guided animal health support through booking, chat, video consultation, prescriptions, and follow-up records.

VetBondhu is for animals only. MediBondhu is the separate human healthcare module and must not share VetBondhu routes, tables, colors, query keys, or medical workflows.

---

## Purpose

VetBondhu exists to make veterinary help easier to reach, especially when an animal owner needs fast first guidance, remote review, or follow-up advice.

The service helps with:

- Early guidance before a condition becomes severe
- Better communication between animal owners and vets
- Video-based review of symptoms, wounds, movement, breathing, feeding, and behavior
- Preserved consultation history for future reference
- Prescription and advice records after a consultation
- Support for livestock, pets, poultry, and farm animals

VetBondhu does not replace hands-on emergency veterinary treatment. Severe or life-threatening cases need the nearest in-person vet, clinic, or animal hospital immediately.

---

## Why VetBondhu

Many animal health problems start with small signs: less eating, fever, coughing, diarrhea, limping, swelling, injury, unusual behavior, or reduced production. Owners may not know if the situation is urgent, what information a vet needs, or how to explain the problem clearly.

VetBondhu gives owners a structured way to:

- Find veterinary support from a digital workspace
- Share animal details before the consultation
- Show the animal through video when needed
- Ask follow-up questions in the same consultation context
- Keep prescriptions and advice accessible
- Reduce confusion between owner observations and vet instructions

For vets, VetBondhu creates a professional channel for consultation requests, waiting-room handling, video calls, prescriptions, and consultation records.

## Before Starting a Consultation

Good preparation helps the vet understand the animal's condition faster.

### Animal details

Prepare:

- Animal type: cow, calf, goat, sheep, chicken, dog, cat, bird, etc.
- Age or approximate age
- Sex and pregnancy/lactation status if relevant
- Weight or approximate body size
- Breed if known
- Farm or home environment
- Number of affected animals

### Problem details

Write down:

- Main problem or symptom
- When the problem started
- Whether the symptom is improving, worsening, or unchanged
- Eating and drinking behavior
- Urination and stool condition
- Fever, coughing, breathing difficulty, vomiting, diarrhea, limping, swelling, wound, bleeding, or weakness
- Any recent injury, transport, feed change, weather stress, or contact with sick animals

### Health history

Keep ready:

- Current medicines or injections already given
- Previous disease history
- Vaccination history
- Deworming history
- Recent delivery, pregnancy, milk production, or breeding details when relevant
- Photos, videos, lab reports, or previous prescriptions if available

### Video call preparation

Before joining the room:

- Charge the phone or laptop
- Keep internet stable
- Use a quiet and safe place
- Make sure there is enough light
- Keep the animal visible but do not stress or force it
- Ask another person to help hold the camera if needed
- Keep the animal's food, water, stool, wound, or affected body part visible if the vet asks

## How to Start Counseling or Consultation

VetBondhu consultation should start with clear and simple information.

1. Select the VetBondhu service and choose an available vet.
2. Describe the animal and the main problem in plain language.
3. Add symptom details, photos, or videos when possible.
4. Confirm the consultation request and join the waiting room.
5. When the vet starts the room, join the video consultation.
6. Begin with the most important issue: "My cow has not eaten since yesterday" or "My dog is breathing fast after injury."
7. Answer the vet's questions carefully.
8. Show the animal, movement, wound, breathing, mouth, eyes, stool, or affected area only when the vet asks.
9. Follow the prescription, dosage, care advice, and follow-up instructions.

Do not hide medicines already given. Incorrect or incomplete medicine history can make the consultation unsafe.

---

## During the Video Call

Use the video call to help the vet observe safely.

Best practices:

- Keep the camera steady.
- Show the full animal first, then the affected area.
- Show breathing from the side if breathing is the issue.
- Show walking only if the animal can move safely.
- Do not force a lame, injured, pregnant, or weak animal to stand.
- Do not put your hand near the mouth, horn, hoof, or wound if the animal may react aggressively.
- Keep children away from large or stressed animals during the call.
- If the animal is dangerous, keep distance and explain the situation verbally.

The vet may ask for close-up video, still photos, temperature, feed details, stool condition, or recent medicine details.

## Animal Emergency Guidelines

Use VetBondhu for first guidance, but do not wait for an online consultation if the animal is in immediate danger. Contact the nearest veterinarian, clinic, or animal hospital immediately.

### General emergency signs

Seek urgent help if the animal has:

- Severe breathing difficulty
- Heavy bleeding
- Deep wound or open fracture
- Poisoning or suspected poisoning
- Continuous seizures
- Sudden collapse or unconsciousness
- Extreme weakness and inability to stand
- Severe dehydration
- Swollen abdomen with pain or breathing distress
- High fever with rapid worsening
- Severe allergic reaction or swelling of face/throat

### Cattle, buffalo, goats, and sheep

Urgent signs include:

- Bloat or rapidly swollen left abdomen
- Not eating with repeated attempts to lie down and stand up
- Difficult delivery or stuck calf/kid/lamb
- Milk fever signs after delivery: weakness, cold ears, inability to stand
- Bloody diarrhea or severe watery diarrhea
- Sudden drop in milk with fever and painful udder
- Severe lameness, hoof injury, or fracture
- Snake bite or suspected poison plant intake
- Heat stress: heavy panting, drooling, weakness, collapse

### Poultry and birds

Urgent signs include:

- Many birds sick or dead in a short time
- Severe breathing sounds, gasping, or blue comb
- Bloody droppings
- Paralysis or twisted neck
- Sudden drop in egg production with sickness
- Severe dehydration or inability to stand

### Dogs, cats, and pets

Urgent signs include:

- Road accident or fall injury
- Difficulty breathing
- Repeated vomiting or diarrhea with weakness
- Not urinating or crying while trying to urinate
- Seizures
- Poison ingestion, chemical exposure, or snake bite
- Eye injury
- Heat stroke signs: heavy panting, drooling, collapse
- Severe bite wound or uncontrolled bleeding

### What owners can do while waiting for help

- Keep the animal calm and safe.
- Move it away from traffic, water, fire, sharp objects, or aggressive animals.
- Do not give human medicine unless a vet instructs it.
- Do not force-feed a weak, choking, bloated, or unconscious animal.
- For bleeding, apply gentle pressure with clean cloth if safe.
- For suspected poisoning, keep the poison container/feed sample for the vet.
- For birth difficulty, do not pull forcefully without veterinary instruction.
- For fracture, limit movement and wait for professional help.

## After Consultation

After the VetBondhu consultation:

- Save or review the prescription.
- Follow dosage, timing, route, and duration exactly.
- Complete the full course if the vet instructs it.
- Monitor eating, drinking, stool, breathing, temperature, and behavior.
- Keep photos or notes of progress.
- Schedule a follow-up if the vet asks.
- Contact the vet again if symptoms worsen or new symptoms appear.
- Seek in-person help immediately if emergency signs appear after the online consultation.

## Admin Operations

VetBondhu admin operations are managed separately from MediBondhu:

- `/admin/vetbondhu-overview` shows VetBondhu overview stats, all vets, currently available vets, recent bookings, active sessions, and vet withdrawals.
- The `Available Now` stat opens the currently online vet list and should use VetBondhu heartbeat/online-window behavior.
- VetBondhu payout/withdrawal review uses VetBondhu veterinary withdrawal data only.
- VetBondhu access controls must not affect MediBondhu doctors, MediBondhu patients, or global account state.

---

## Safety and Responsibility

VetBondhu supports remote veterinary guidance and consultation. It helps owners organize information, communicate with vets, and follow professional advice.

It should not be used to delay emergency care. Some conditions require physical examination, injections, surgery, lab tests, fluid therapy, delivery assistance, or hospital-level treatment.

Animal owners are responsible for sharing accurate information. Vets are responsible for giving professional advice within the limits of remote consultation.
